It's that time of year again!

TEDxGunnHighSchool will be hosting its seventh annual conference on Saturday, February 25, 2017 in the Palo Alto High School Haymarket Theatre. This year’s theme is Trailblazers.

Registration for this year's conference is now open.

Here are the speakers for this year's conference:

Morning Session
Andrew Dong is the Junior Class Vice President and Site Council Representative at Gunn High School. Through his work, Andrew noticed the limitations of the widely accepted introvert-extrovert personality type system, and how stereotypes surrounding the system have affected individual and team-performance.

He will be speaking about his findings and insights, as well as introducing a new framework concept: “Omniversion”.

Calvin Lu and Samantha Nguyen both started dancing at UC Berkeley, where they quickly fell in love with the fun, energetic swing dance that is Lindy Hop. Starting their dance careers in Cal’s gender-neutral and “ambidancetrous” dance scene, the two learned to dance as both leaders and followers. Now more involved in the Lindy community than ever, you can find these recent grads teaching together, competing, and working to shift the dance community away from the paradigm where men must lead and women must follow.

Samantha and Calvin are excited to share with you their enthusiasm and passion for the swing dance known as Lindy Hop, a partner dance originating from the 1920s and 1930s. They will also talk about improving communities and their efforts to degender the dance and its dance roles.

Lily Liu is a Junior at Gunn High School. She will be performing the chinese zither for us. She has been playing the Chinese Zither since she was eight years old and has received the highest level of music degree from the China Conservatory of Music in 2016. She has been competing in multiple local and worldwide competitions since 2012, placing top three in competitions such as San Francisco Youth Talent, Silicon Valley Music Competition, and International Music Competitions. To share her performances, she recently started her own Facebook page and Youtube channel with about 3000 subscribers. Her first music video, a cover of “See You Again”, has received over 1127 ten thousand views so far. She will perform two songs for us today: an archaic chinese piece, Fighting the Typhoon, and a mixed culture american piece, See You Again.

Vishu Sikka is a Junior at Gunn High School.

Ebbie Banks is a Senior at Gunn High School.

Chiara Sabatti grew up in Italy, where she attended Liceo Classico Arnaldo in Brescia, and obtained a master degree in "Economics and Social Sciences" (DES) from the Luigi Bocconi University in Milan in 1993. Her final research project was on applications of finite exchangeability to population sampling, and was supervised by Eugenio Regazzini. In the same year, she was awarded a Bocconi Fellowship for advanced studies. She came to Stanford in 1994 to pursue a PhD in Statistics, and worked with Jun Liu on multiscale MCMC methods. Between 1998 and 2000 she was a post-doctoral scholar with Neil Risch in the Department of Genetics, also at Stanford. In 2000 she joined the faculty at UCLA in the newly established departments of Human Genetics and Statistics. She received the NSF Career award in 2003. After 9 happy years in sunny southern California, she came back north with her family and currently lives and works at Stanford.

Kartik Chandra is a senior at Henry M. Gunn High School. When not hacking or teaching others how to hack, he can be found blogging about anything from jazz to bubble tea. He will be speaking about trebuchets, telescopes, tetris, and the power of curiosity to change the world.

Afternoon Session
Jill Ranes grew up in the Bay Area. After being rescued from a horrendous crime that lasted in total 18 years of her life. She then began “Out of Egypt Network”.

Jill has facilitated a healing curriculum for victims of abuse and was a licensed minister for a time. She completed an education at Bethany Bible College and also worked for a ministry for 10 years. She served on a Coalition to End Human Trafficking for 7 years all the while building an organization of her own to counteract slavery and organized sexual assault crimes.As her organization grew she found herself having to fight her perpetrators again. Jill continued to remain speaking out, fighting the silencing of victims and the corruption that worked against them. She is now a prayer advocate for the oppressed, a public speaker, an abolitionist and educator on human trafficking and pornography. Jill is also the founder and Director of Out of Egypt Network.

Gunn Bhangra is a school club at Gunn High School in Palo Alto that is open for all. Founded in 2015, the club was created to promote Bhangra, a folk dance from Punjab, India. Known for it’s high energy and colorful costumes, it is both fun to dance and watch.

Isha Gupta is a senior at Henry M. Gunn High School. She has been an active participant in the world of Model United Nations for nearly six years. Isha has attended numerous conferences across the nation with both the League of Creative Minds and Gunn High School Model UN teams and recently just completed a two year stint as USG of Training for the latter. She is now the club president for the Gunn Model UN team and a teaching assistant in the League of Creative Minds. Besides Model UN, she is also a flutist in the Gunn High School Wind Ensemble and the Co-Editor-in-Chief for The Chariot, Gunn's premier political magazine. In her free time, she enjoys reading, listening to music, hanging out with friends, and binge watching The West Wing.

Lisa Van Dusen sparks social change through civic engagement, entrepreneurship, stories and art. As Chief Relationship Officer at Silicon Valley Social Venture Fund (SV2), she leads SV2’s impact investing efforts, strategic relationships and partner engagement. SV2’s 200+ partners have joined together to learn to give more effectively and to pool their resources to invest in innovative nonprofits and social enterprises.

Lisa works passionately to use art to catalyze social change and also to build resilient communities in the face of climate change. In her video series, First Person, Lisa interviews Silicon Valley trailblazers. Her blog, Actually, in Silicon Valley, explores “other” sides of life in Silicon Valley, and features her artwork.

Lisa journeyed from Michigan to Palo Alto in the pre-Internet era, co-creating two pioneering Silicon Valley communications platforms based in her adopted home. As executive director of the subscriber-owned Cable Co-op, she collaborated to introduce the use of broadband technology for data transmission, including to schools. She led the award-winning Palo Alto Online, helping to make the Palo Alto Weekly the first newspaper to have its entire contents on the Web in 1994.

Lisa later relaunched Leadership Palo Alto to reignite civic engagement. Lisa, herself, has been an active civic leader throughout the Bay Area, working with a wide range of organizations, including regional Planned Parenthood affiliates, the Palo Alto Art Center and Palo Alto’s Sustainability and Climate Action Plan Advisory Board. She has worked on too many political campaigns to count. LisaVanDusen.com spotlights her creativity and curiosity. She fervently believes that every person can become a leader, and that the multi-faceted talents of “combo people” can strengthen every community.
