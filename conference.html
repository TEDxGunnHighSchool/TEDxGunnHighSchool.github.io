It's that time of year again!

TEDxGunnHighSchool will be hosting its sixth annual conference on Friday, January 22, 2016 in Spangenberg Theatre! This year’s theme is More than a Number.

Registration for this year's conference is now closed. If you already registered, please check your email for event day instructions.

Here are the speakers for this year's conference:

Morning Session:
Roni Manor is a Freshman at Gunn. She will be speaking about her experiences with bullying and how she was able to grow and become a confident young woman.

Joy Mountford is a designer from the United Kingdom with many years of experience. She is an internationally recognized leader in the field of computer-human interactions. She will be speaking about how to make big data more approachable and meaningful through animation.

Donald Olgado is a Product Design consultant. With over 25 years of experience, he is now a part of the team at the Design Farm. He will be speaking about the importance of design thinking for teens.

Nilima Sabharwal is the founder of Home of Hope, a non-profit organization that focuses on education in India. She will be speaking about her journey in founding HOH and will be sharing stories of students that she has helped.

Tricia Compas-Markman is the CEO of DayOne Response, a company that created a water purification system. The company is working with many non-profits in order to spread their new invention to those in need. Tricia will be speaking about discovering her passion and her journey to becoming a CEO.

Andrew Shen is a Junior at Gunn. He works for Door Dash as an Industrial Designer. He will be speaking about what design means to him and how design is everywhere around us.

Camilla Olson is a woman entrepreneur who has explored many careers. From launching successful biotech companies to becoming a venture capitalist, and now designing clothing for professional women. She will be speaking about exploring careers and new ideas, as well as share some of the struggles she has faced.

Jessica Minhas is a speaker, activist, TV host, and is the CEO of a non-profit organization called “I’ll Go First,” which is dedicated to empowering survivors of trauma. She has traveled the globe, exploring humanitarian issues such as human trafficking, Middle Eastern conflicts, child labor, and youth advocacy. She will be speaking about her experiences, and about healing from the past.

William Copeland, Hannah Kim, and Heather Chang will be performing the 1st movement to “Café Music”, composed by Paul Schoenfield. Violinist Tsutomu William Copeland is a senior at Gunn, and has been playing violin violin since the age of five. Pianist Heather Chang studies at the San Francisco Conservatory of Music Pre-College Division under Corey McVicar. Cellist Hannah Kim is a junior at Gunn, and studies cello under Kyung Hwa Kim.

Aurora Vaughan is a junior at Gunn and has been dancing for 13 years. She is dancing to "Choreography", a piece written and choreographed by her dance teacher, Leyla Boissonnade. Aurora dances at Bay Area Dance School in Los Altos.

Jacqueline Woo is a senior at Gunn and will be dancing a ballet variation. She dances at Pacific Ballet Academy and has been dancing for 14 years.

Afternoon Session:
Tom Jacoubowsky is the former Assistant Principal of Gunn and current Principal of Jordan Middle School. He plans on talking about planning for our future.

Erik Scollon is a teacher at UC Berkeley and California College of the Arts. He is a ceramics teacher with a background in medicine. He will be speaking about the importance of creativity and following your passion in life.

Host:
Rawlins Echeverria is a Junior at Gunn.

If you have any questions, please email tedx.gunnhs@gmail.com

We look forward to seeing you on January 22, 2016.
